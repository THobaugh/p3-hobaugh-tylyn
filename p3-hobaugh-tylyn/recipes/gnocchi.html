Entrees | 12.12.20

The quick and healthy gnocchi recipe for your busy weekdays.

My family and I have been enjoying this dip for years, thanks to our neighbor. With some slight tweeks to make it more our style, it has become a once a month starter that may even be at a holiday gathering. The dressing packs in a lot of flavor, and feel free to adjust the heat to your liking.

Ingredients:

  • 1 package of Gnocchi
  • 1 bag of Brussel Sprouts (Fresh is preferred)
  • 1 carton of Baby Bella Mushrooms
  • 1 Sweet Onion
  • 1 1/2 tbsp minced Garlic
  • 1/2 tsp Red Pepper
  • 3 tsp Honey
  • 2 tsp Lemon Juice
  • 3 tbsp Butter
  • pinch of Salt
  • pinch of Black Pepper
  • 1 1/2 tbsp Olive Oil

Instructions:

  1. Bring a pot of Salt water to a boil.
  2. While the water is heating up, halve the Brussel Sprouts, and cut the Sweet Onion into slices.
  3. Place the Brussel Sprouts and Sweet Onion into a pan with the Olive Oil.
  4. Place the Mushrooms in a smaller pan to sauté in the Butter and minced Garlic. Stir occasionally.
  5. Add your Red Pepper, Salt, and Black Pepper to your Brussel Sprouts and Sweet Onions.
  6. Once the Brussel Sprouts have a slight light brown look, and the Sweet Onions have a slight crunch, check on your mushrooms. If the mushrooms are sautéed, add to the Brussel Sprouts and Sweet Onion pan, and turn the stove to a lower level.
  7. At this point, the water should be boiling. Add the Gnocchi to the water.
  8. In about 5 minutes the Gnocchi should float to the top, which means it is cooked.
  9. Take the cooked Gnocchi out of the pot and place into a serving dish.
  10. Top the Gnocchi with the Brussel Sprouts, Sween Onion, and Mushrooms.
